BITCOIN & THE BIRTH OF THE BLOCKCHAIN

In 2013 Bitcoin took the investment world by storm and introduced a new type of distributed network: the Blockchain.

A Bitcoin developer named Vitalik believed blockchain technology could benefit other applications beyond cryptocurrency. Ethereum was born with the goal of bringing blockchain technology to the mass public.

ETHEREUM DIFFERENCES

  • Platform

    Think of Bitcoin as the cellphone and Ethereum as the iPhone. Ethereum allows developers to build applications on top of their platform to function as more than a currency.

  • Decentralization

    ASIC mining has made Bitcoin a highly centralized network consisting of large scale mining farms. Ethereum is ASIC resistant by design to discourage centralization.

  • Smart Contracts

    Smart contracts can be executed and enforced automatically over the Ethereum network without the need for a trusted third party and their fees.

RISE & FALL OF THE DAO

The DAO was an autonomous venture capital fund and the most notable application built on top of Ethereum. In May, 2016 it raised over $150MM in the largest crowdfunding campaign in history. Only a month later, a vulnerability in the DAO code was exploited to siphon off over $50MM of the DAO's funds.

In response, the Ethereum network unanimously voted to implement a hard fork to handle the recovery of the lost funds. The vulnerability existed within the DAO code and not Ethereum itself and serves as a cautionary tale for application developers. We believe this lesson will ultimately lead to a stronger network and more secure applications moving forward.
